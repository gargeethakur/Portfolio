A Comprehensive Study of Quick Hull: Algorithm, Complexity and Applications

Status: Submitted for publication to IEEE

This paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the QuickHull algorithm for computing convex hulls, comparing it with other methods like Graham’s Scan, Jarvis’s March, and Monotone Chain in terms of algorithm design, complexity, and performance. It highlights QuickHull’s average-case efficiency, practical applications, and limitations, particularly under circular point distributions and floating-point inaccuracies.
